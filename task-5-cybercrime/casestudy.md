UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime in India, where fraudsters trick users into transferring money through deceptive methods. These scams often rely on creating urgency and exploiting a lack of awareness among users.

A typical case involves a college student receiving a phone call from someone pretending to be a bank representative or customer support executive. The caller informs the student about a supposed issue with their bank account, such as it being blocked or a reward pending. To resolve the issue, the fraudster sends a UPI collect request and instructs the student to approve it, claiming that it will help receive money or fix the problem. Trusting the caller, the student approves the request, which instead results in money being deducted from their account.

In some cases, fraudsters also send fake links or QR codes, misleading users into thinking they will receive money. However, scanning these codes or clicking such links actually authorizes a payment. Young users and students are often targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI transactions work.

The consequences include financial loss, panic, stress, and difficulty in recovering the money. Victims may also lose trust in digital systems. This highlights the importance of awareness, caution, and responsible use of digital payment systems.
